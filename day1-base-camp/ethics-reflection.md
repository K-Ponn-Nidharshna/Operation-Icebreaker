# Security Ethics — Field Code Reflection
**Operation IceBreaker | Day 1 | K. Ponn Nidharshna**

Security ethics refers to the responsible and lawful use of 
cybersecurity knowledge and tools. Cybersecurity professionals must 
respect privacy, follow laws, and obtain proper authorization before 
testing or accessing any system.

Key ethical principles include:

1. **Authorization First** – Never access, scan, or test systems 
   without explicit permission.
2. **Protect Privacy** – Respect and safeguard sensitive information.
3. **Do No Harm** – Use cybersecurity skills to protect systems, 
   not to cause damage.
4. **Act Responsibly** – Report vulnerabilities through proper 
   channels instead of exploiting them.
5. **Follow Laws and Policies** – Comply with legal and organizational 
   security requirements.
6. **Maintain Integrity** – Be honest, trustworthy, and professional 
   in all security activities.
